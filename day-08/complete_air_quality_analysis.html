🔍 Root Causes of Air Pollution in Pakistan
🚗 Vehicular Emissions (40%)
Rapid urbanization, old vehicles, fuel quality. Major contributor in metropolitan areas like Lahore, Karachi, Peshawar.
🏭 Industrial Pollution (25%)
Faisalabad textile industry, cement factories, brick kilns. Concentrated in industrial belt cities.
🌾 Crop Burning (20%)
October-December stubble burning in Punjab. Creates severe smog episodes, AQI can reach 300+.
🏗️ Construction Dust (10%)
Rapid urban construction, poor emission controls. Affects all major cities.
🔥 Waste Burning (5%)
Open burning of municipal/medical waste. Contributes heavy metals and toxic compounds.

💨 PM2.5 vs PM10 Analysis
PM2.5 (Fine Particulates)
PM10 (Coarse Particulates)
Key Differences:
  • PM2.5 (Fine): 2.5 micrometers - penetrates deep into lungs & bloodstream. More dangerous. Current avg: 18.4 µg/m³ (WHO limit: 5 µg/m³)
  • PM10 (Coarse): 10 micrometers - deposits in upper respiratory tract. Current avg: 35.2 µg/m³ (WHO limit: 15 µg/m³)
  • Faisalabad PM2.5: 25 µg/m³ = 5x WHO limit (SEVERE)
  • Danger Zone: When PM2.5 > 25 µg/m³, respiratory symptoms increase dramatically
✅ Comprehensive Health Recommendations
For Current AQI (Moderate 51-100):
  • Wear N95/KN95 masks during morning (6-9 AM) & evening (5-8 PM) peak hours
  • Limit outdoor activities to 30 minutes. Avoid intense exercise outdoors
  • Use HEPA air purifiers (6-8 hours/day minimum) indoors
  • Keep windows closed during peak pollution hours
  • Monitor air quality before planning outdoor activities
  • Increase intake of antioxidants (fruits, vegetables, green tea)
  • Stay hydrated - drink 3-4 liters water daily to help lungs
  • Install indoor air monitors in homes & offices
For High-Risk Groups (Always):
  • Children: Limit outdoor play during peak hours. Use masks during commute to school
  • Elderly: Stay indoors during moderate AQI. Medical check-ups quarterly
  • Asthma/COPD Patients: Carry rescue inhalers always. Consult doctors before outdoor activity
  • Pregnant Women: Exposure linked to low birth weight. Use air purifiers. Limit outdoor time
  • Cardiovascular Patients: High risk of heart attacks during poor AQI. Stay home during high pollution
Long-term Protective Measures:
  • Install whole-home HEPA filtration system (₹30,000-50,000)
  • Use exhaust fans while cooking to remove indoor pollutants
  • Maintain car air filters - change every 15,000 km
  • Grow indoor air-purifying plants (Snake plant, Pothos, Areca palm)
  • Get annual lung function tests if in high-pollution cities
  • Participate in tree-planting initiatives - each tree filters 10 kg/year pollution
  • Support policies for vehicle emission standards & industrial regulations

🎯 Quick Action Guide by AQI Level
AQI 0-50: Go outside freely. Exercise without masks. No precautions needed.
AQI 51-100: Use mask (sensitive), limit intense outdoor exercise, use air purifier 6+ hours/day, close windows during peak hours.
AQI 101-150: N95 mask mandatory, avoid outdoor exercise, work from home if possible, run air purifier 24/7, medical check for high-risk groups.
AQI 151-200: STAY INDOORS, seal home, N95 masks, hospital standby for high-risk, postpone outdoor activities, emergency medical support.
AQI 201+: HAZARDOUS EMERGENCY, remain indoors, run all purifiers, close all windows/doors, seek medical help for symptoms, avoid travel.
